  • # 2023.06.20

As organizations expand, the escalating costs of communication and coordination become evident.
Effective information sharing and action coordination are crucial but come at a price of time
and resources. Group meetings can reduce individual communications, provided there is sufficient
overlap among groups. Balancing efficient groups with minimizing participants in communication
loops is vital. Additionally, hidden costs persist in informal communications, necessitating
ongoing analysis. Managing communication and coordination is essential for organizations as they
grow, requiring careful attention and continuous efforts to optimize efficiency.

  • # 2023.06.19

One reason for unnecessary meetings and status updates is the need for organizational leaders to
stay informed about ongoing tasks. While this awareness is important, the current methods, such
as frequent meetings, Slack messages, and hallway conversations, actually decrease productivity.
A better approach as the organization grows is for teams to regularly share status updates as
part of their normal workflow. Leaders can then read these updates at their convenience and
engage in synchronous discussions only when necessary. This way, synchronous meetings can be
reserved for immediate collaboration on complex issues, and collaboration itself can be focused within those meetings.
